  1. Pediatrics
    Resting-State Functional Connectivity in a Community Sample of Children With a Range of Cognitive Disengagement Syndrome Symptoms
    Stephen P. Becker, Adebayo Braimah, Jonathan A. Dudley, and 2 more authors
    JAACAP Open, 2024
    Abs DOI

    ABSTRACT Objective Despite rapid advancements in our understanding of cognitive disengagement syndrome (CDS), less is known about the neural correlates of CDS. The objective was to examine associations between children’s CDS symptom severity and connectivity within and between specific brain networks. Method Sixty-five right-handed children (ages 8-13 years; 36 boys) with the full continuum of CDS symptom severity were recruited from the community. As part of a cross-sectional descriptive study investigating CDS, children underwent 10-minute resting state functional magnetic resonance imaging. Connectivity metrics were extracted from the default mode network (DMN) and ventral and dorsal attention networks. Parents and teachers completed measures of CDS and attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder inattention (ADHD-IN) symptoms. Multivariate parametric cluster analyses were performed on within- and between-network connections of the specified networks, with age and sex included as covariates. Separate models were conducted with and without controlling for ADHD-IN symptom severity. Results Parent-rated CDS symptom severity was not significantly associated with any between- or within-networks associations of interest. When ADHD-IN symptom severity was included in the model, teacher-rated CDS symptom severity was associated with greater functional connectivity between several regions of the DMN and ventral attention network. Conclusion This study builds on theoretical and empirical evidence suggesting atypical connectivity between task positive and task negative networks as potentially key for understanding the neural correlates of CDS. These findings are important for building a neuroscience-based understanding of CDS and support emerging theoretical models linking CDS to mind wandering as well as DMN-related dysfunction.

  2. Biomedical
    Effects of Omega-3 Fatty Acids on Brain Connectivity in Long-Evans Rats
    In , 2019
    Abs

    This study examines the impact of dietary fatty acid intake on functional connectivity of the maturing brain. This study was performed with 88 rats. In vivo as well as ex vivo neurological data was collected by means of MR imaging and postmortem gas chromatography. The graphs were compared by using network-based statistics and showed non-significant trends between the controls and the fatty-acids deficient group. Network metrics were also computed and showed non-significant trends between the controls and the fatty acids deficient group.
